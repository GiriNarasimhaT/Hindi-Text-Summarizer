Text Summarization is a process of reducing the content of a lengthy article or a text document in such a way that the exact meaning that the document wants to convey, is not lost. Many improvements have been made in the field of text summarization for English, which is the most spoken language in the world. But the demand for text summarization methods for local or native languages is increasing in recent times. Compared to English, researchers face various challenges while implementing a text summarization system for Indian languages, because of the diversity and linguistically rich texts. We chose to implement an Automatic Text Summarization (ATS) system for Hindi language as it is the second most spoken language in India and third most spoken language in the world. There are very few advancements when it comes to an efficient text summarization system for this language.

As an enormous amount of text data is available on the internet, it has become a difficult task to grasp important information out of it. The difficulty becomes more in the case of local or native languages. In such cases, an Automatic Text Summarization (ATS) system is of great help. Text summarization is a process of compressing an actual text article or document by eliminating redundant information or sentences from articles and only keeping those sentences which are actually of great help. This ensures that the reader does not get bored while looking at the size of the articles, and at the same time, the reader also gets the information that he/she is looking for, without the need for reading the whole passage.

In general, text summarization is done using three approaches:
  (i) Numerical based approach
  (ii) Linguistic based approach
  (iii) Hybrid approach.

    Numerical approach involves assigning numerical scores to the sentences in a passage, on the basis of their importance. The sentences with highest scores will be kept in the summary, whereas the sentences with least scores will not be included, considering it to be of no use. Linguistic approach involves generation of summaries by linguists, who are considered to be experts in a particular language. Hybrid approach is a combination of numerical based approach and linguistic based approach.

Text summarization systems are basically of two types:
  (i) Extractive Summarization
  (ii) Abstractive Summarization

Extractive summarization involves extracting important sentences from the article and concatenating them without giving importance to understanding the meaning of them. On the other hand, Abstractive summarization involves generating a meaningful summary. Instead of just picking sentences from original texts, abstractive systems rewrite the sentences whenever necessary. In case of Indian languages, abstractive summarization is not effective as Indian languages are linguistically rich in nature unlike English language. Hence, generation of new words and placing them in a proper position in a sentence is a difficult task in case of Abstractive summarization of Indian languages. Hence we go for the Extractive way of summarization.
